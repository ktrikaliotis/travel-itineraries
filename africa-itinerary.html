Good to know

Tide times (Zanzibar, local): swim on the east coast (Jambiani, Paje, Michamvi) within ~2 hours of high tide; at low tide the sea recedes up to ~300 m — that's reef-walk and seaweed-farm time. Nungwi and Kendwa swim at any tide.

Reading the pattern: highs shift ~30 min later each day. Morning ocean swims are best right after breakfast (06:30–08:30); midday is always low; the evening high rises through dinner — which is exactly why the 18:00 Rock sitting on Mon 17 works (water back in, boat crossing, sunset 18:20).

Money: carry USD cash (small, post-2013 bills) and Tanzanian shillings; cards work in bigger hotels only. Negotiate boat trips.

Bring: reef-safe sunscreen, water shoes, motion-sickness tablets for the Mnemba boat, light long sleeves for Stone Town evenings.

Good to know

Sheldrick timing is fixed: the elephant orphanage is open to the public only 11:00–12:00 daily — there's no way to shift this, so both Day 2 and Day 3 are built around fixed or preferred windows rather than early starts.

Kibera etiquette: go with a resident-led guide only, ask before photographing anyone, dress modestly, wear closed shoes, and route any giving (clothes, sweets, money) through the guide or the school the tour supports rather than handing things to children directly.

Altitude: Nairobi sits at ~1,700 m — mornings can be cool. Bring a light layer for Friday's Naivasha drive.

Payments: Giraffe Centre takes card/M-Pesa only, no cash; carry some KES cash for the Kibera guide, tips, craft stalls, and Sheldrick.

Driving: same rules as Zanzibar — left-hand traffic; if self-driving, roads into Karen and toward Naivasha are well-marked tarmac, but a private driver/guide for Days 2–3 removes the parking and navigation hassle.

Confirm all times locally — hours, tides, and showtimes can shift seasonally
